# Welcome to Liberty 1776!

Hey Andy! Ready to become a hero of the American Revolution? In **Liberty 1776**, you'll command an army during the biggest independence battle in American history (1775-1783). You can fight for either the **Patriots** (the American independence fighters) or the **British** (the Royal Army). The battles are intense, the cards are powerful, and the fate of a nation rests in your hands!

This guide will teach you everything you need to know to dominate every battle. Let's go!

---

## The Two Sides: Patriots vs. British

**The Patriots** are fighting for American independence! Led by the legendary **George Washington**, they include militia members, sharpshooters, and French allies. They rely on quick units and clever tactics.

**The British** are the mighty Royal Army! Led by the skilled **Lord Cornwallis**, they field well-trained Redcoats, elite Grenadiers, and hired Hessian soldiers. They're strong, disciplined, and dangerous.

Each side has its own unique cards and strategy. Which side will you choose?

## How to Play

### Starting the Game
1. **Open the game** on your device (iPad, computer, or phone)
2. **Pick your faction:** Click either "Patriots" or "British" at the menu
3. **You're in!** Your enemy is chosen by the game, and the battle begins

### Your Turn (The Basic Flow)

When it's your turn, here's what you can do:

1. **Play cards** from your hand (the cards at the bottom of your screen)
2. **Attack with your units** by clicking them and attacking enemy units or the enemy hero
3. **Click "End Turn"** when you're done

That's it! Simple but strategic.

### Understanding Supply

**Supply** is your mana or energy system. It's like your power budget each turn.

- **You start with 1 supply** at the beginning of the game
- **Your supply grows by 1 each turn** (so turn 2 = 2 supply, turn 3 = 3 supply, etc.)
- **Maximum supply is 8** (you can't go higher, even if you earn more)
- **Every card costs supply to play** — bigger, stronger cards cost more supply
- **Some cards add extra supply** when you play them (look for the "+1 supply" effect on heroes!)

Pro tip! Don't waste your supply early. Save it for powerful cards when you need them most.

### Types of Cards

Every card in your deck falls into one of three categories:

#### Units (Soldiers)
**Units are your army!** They fight on the battlefield. Every unit has:
- **Attack (⚔)** — How much damage they deal when they attack
- **Health (❤)** — How much damage they can take before they die
- **Examples:** Minuteman (2/1 means 2 attack, 1 health), Continental Regular (4/4)

Units stay on the field once you play them and fight for you every turn.

#### Heroes (Legendary Warriors)
**Heroes are SUPER powerful units!** They're like units but even better:
- They have **higher attack and health** than regular units
- They have a **special Battle Cry effect** that triggers when you play them
- **Examples:** George Washington has a Battle Cry that draws 2 extra cards!

Heroes are expensive (they cost a lot of supply) but absolutely worth it.

#### Orders (Commands & Events)
**Orders are instant effects!** They happen right away and then disappear:
- They don't stay on the field — they activate and vanish
- They can deal damage, draw cards, gain supply, or other cool effects
- **Examples:** "Crossing the Delaware" deals 3 damage instantly

Orders are great for timing your attacks or getting resources you need.

### How Combat Works

**Attacking with Units:**
1. Click one of YOUR units on the field
2. Click an ENEMY unit (to attack that unit) OR click directly on the enemy if they have NO units on the field (to attack their HP directly)
3. **Both sides take damage!** If your unit has 5 attack and their unit has 3 attack, your unit takes 3 damage and their unit takes 5 damage

**Direct Attacks:**
- If the enemy has NO units on the field, your units can attack the **enemy hero directly**
- This is your path to victory!

**Death:**
- When a unit's health reaches 0, it dies and leaves the field
- You can then attack the enemy hero directly with your other units

### Winning the Game

**Your goal:** Reduce the enemy's HP to 0!

The enemy hero starts with **20 HP**. Every attack that gets through will chip away at their health. The first one to reach 0 loses!

## Strategy Tips for Winning

Now that you know the cards, here are 5 awesome tips to help you dominate:

### Tip 1: Play Cheap Cards Early
In the first few turns, you don't have much supply. Play your 1-cost and 2-cost cards early to build your army. Don't waste supply on expensive cards when you can't use them yet!

### Tip 2: Save Heroes for When You're Powerful
Heroes are incredible but expensive. Wait until you have 5+ supply so you can play them AND attack on the same turn. A hero that sits unused is a wasted card.

### Tip 3: Trade Weak Units Into Strong Ones
If your 2/3 Patriot Militia fights their 4/2 Frontier Rifleman, you win the trade (your unit survives with 1 health, their unit dies). Always look for good trades!

### Tip 4: Use Orders to Break Through
When the enemy has units blocking your path to their hero, use an Order card to damage them without using your units. Then your units can attack the hero directly!

### Tip 5: Draw Cards Wins Games
Cards give you options and power. Many heroes and orders draw cards. Use them! The more cards you have, the more options you have each turn.

### Pro Tip: Know When to Attack the Hero Directly
Once the enemy has no units on the field, stop attacking units and start attacking their hero. Every attack counts toward the win!

## The American Revolution: A Kid's Guide

### What Was the American Revolution?

Imagine you live in a country, but you're ruled by a king far, far away who makes all the decisions. You pay taxes to HIM, but you have no say in what happens. You can't even vote! That's how the American colonists felt in 1776.

The American colonies — places like Massachusetts, Virginia, and New York — were run by Britain and King George III. For over 100 years, this was fine. But after 1765, the British started taxing everything: stamps, tea, paper, even glass! The colonists said, "We don't want to pay these taxes unless we get a vote!" The King said no. So the colonists decided to fight for independence.

From 1775 to 1783, Americans fought the British Army for their freedom. It was a HUGE war with thousands of soldiers, naval battles, and famous generals. In the end, the Americans won and created the United States of America — the country you live in today!

### Key Events of the Revolution

**1765 — The Stamp Act**
The King taxes stamps. Colonists are furious and say "No taxation without representation!" (They want a vote first.)

**1775 — Battles of Lexington & Concord**
The first shots of the war are fired in Massachusetts. The Revolution officially begins!

**1776 — Declaration of Independence**
On July 4th, the colonists officially declare that America is a FREE, independent country. (That's why we celebrate the 4th of July!)

**1777 — Battle of Saratoga**
America wins a huge battle! France sees that America can actually beat the British, so they decide to join the war and help.

**1778 — France Enters the War**
French soldiers, ships, and money pour into America. Now America isn't alone!

**1781 — Battle of Yorktown**
In Virginia, the American and French armies trap the British General Cornwallis. He surrenders! The war is basically over.

**1783 — Treaty of Paris**
Britain officially accepts that America is independent. The war is over, and America is FREE!

**Legacy:** The American Revolution inspired people all over the world to fight for freedom and independence. It created a new nation based on the idea that all people are equal and deserve to have a say in their government.

A menu will pop up with lots of options. Scroll down a bit. You'll see **"Add to Home Screen"** — tap it!

**Step 5: Name Your App**
A small window will appear with a text field. It probably already says "Liberty 1776" but you can change it if you want. Keep it simple!

**Step 6: Tap "Add"**
Hit the blue **"Add"** button in the top-right corner.

**Done!**
Liberty 1776 now appears on your iPad's home screen just like a regular app! Tap it anytime to play. No need to open Safari or search for it ever again — it's right there waiting for you!
